## What is PCORI?

The **Patient-Centered Outcomes Research Institute (PCORI)** is an independent, nonprofit, nongovernmental organization authorized by Congress through the Affordable Care Act of 2010. Its mission is to fund research that helps people make informed healthcare decisions and improves the delivery of care.

PCORI represents a paradigm shift in how clinical research is funded and designed:

| Traditional Research Model | PCORI Model |
|---|---|
| Researchers identify questions | Patients and researchers co-identify questions |
| Patients are subjects | Patients are partners |
| Outcomes defined by clinicians | Outcomes defined collaboratively |
| Results disseminated to journals | Results disseminated to patients and practitioners |
| Research may or may not reflect patient priorities | Patient priorities are the starting point |

## PCORI Engagement Standards

PCORI has developed explicit standards for how patients should be engaged in research — not as token participants, but as genuine partners. These map directly onto the Informatics Stack's **Role** and **Goals** levels.

### The Six PCORI Engagement Principles

1. **Reciprocal relationships** — patients and researchers share power and respect each other's expertise
2. **Co-learning** — both patients and researchers expand their knowledge through the partnership
3. **Partnerships with purpose** — engagement is tied to specific research decisions, not just optics
4. **Transparency** — research processes and decisions are open to patient scrutiny
5. **Honesty** — patients are told when their input is not incorporated, and why
6. **Responsiveness** — research processes adapt based on patient feedback

## Levels of Patient Engagement

PCORI distinguishes several levels of patient engagement in research — from consultation to full partnership:

```
Consultation (lowest)
    ↓
Collaboration
    ↓
Co-Leadership
    ↓
Patient-Led (highest)
```

A study where patients **co-lead** means patients hold decision-making authority over study design, protocol, data analysis, and publication — not just review a completed design.

## The CMSS/PLRC Scorecard Framework

PCORI funded the **Council of Medical Specialty Societies (CMSS)** and the **Patient-Led Research Collaborative (PLRC)** to develop a validated framework for evaluating collaborative research quality: the **Patient-Led Research Scorecard**.

This is covered in detail in the [CMSS Scorecards module](cmss-scorecards.md), but the core insight is:

> *The scorecards move beyond engagement toward a solution where patient-generated data and patient-led outcomes research become an essential component of medical research, leading to more patient-centric comparative effectiveness research.*

Four domains are evaluated:

1. **Patient/Partner Governance** — Is decision-making genuinely shared?
2. **Integration into Research Process** — Are patients involved at every stage?
3. **Patient Burden** — Is patient participation sustainable and compensated fairly?
4. **Research Organization Readiness** — Does the research partner have the infrastructure and culture for meaningful collaboration?

## PCORI-Funded Research Examples

### Rare Epilepsy Network

- PCORI provided a **$3 million grant in 2013** to fund the Rare Epilepsy Network (REN) registry
- REN collected data on over 1,459 patients across 40 rare epilepsy disorders
- Directly enabled patient-centered research on conditions where no clinical trial data existed
- The registry was built with patient advocates and epileptologists co-designing the data elements

### ALS Communication Technology

PCORI funded a **$13 million, five-year study** led by Dartmouth and the Sean M. Healey & AMG Center for ALS at Massachusetts General Hospital to test the effectiveness of **video as a communication tool during clinical visits** for people with ALS and their caregivers.

- 800 total participants across 8 NEALS sites
- Tests whether video recordings of specialist visits — made available to patients to review — improve clinical communication and care outcomes
- Designed with significant input from ALS patient advocates

## What Patient-Led Research Looks Like in Practice

<div class="patient-voice">
"Patient-led research isn't patients playing at being scientists. It's scientists and patients recognizing that lived experience is a form of expertise — and designing research that honors both."
<cite>— Hannah Davis, Patient-Led Research Collaborative, Long COVID researcher</cite>
</div>

The **Patient-Led Research Collaborative (PLRC)** — which grew from the Long COVID patient community — is a model of what fully patient-led research can accomplish:

- Founded and led entirely by patients with Long COVID
- Published peer-reviewed research on symptom profiles, serology, and recovery patterns that preceded and influenced NIH-funded work
- Demonstrated that patient-led research can be scientifically rigorous, not despite patient leadership, but because of it

## Informatics Opportunities in Patient-Centered Research

From an informatics standpoint, PCORI's model creates specific technical needs:

| Patient Partnership Requirement | Informatics Solution |
|---|---|
| Patients co-design outcomes | PRO (patient-reported outcome) development tools |
| Patients contribute data | SMART on FHIR apps, patient registry platforms |
| Patients see study results | Data visualization dashboards accessible to laypeople |
| Patients help disseminate findings | Social media analytics, patient community platforms |
| Patients review data for accuracy | Patient access to their own research record |
