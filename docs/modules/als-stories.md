## The ALS Advocacy Ecosystem

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) is a fatal neurodegenerative disease that progressively destroys motor neurons, ultimately affecting the ability to move, speak, swallow, and breathe. With a median survival of 2–5 years from diagnosis and no curative treatment, ALS patients and families have built one of the most sophisticated patient advocacy ecosystems in rare disease.

The ALS community's approach to informatics and research is a masterclass in applying the Informatics Stack from World down to Technology.

---

## Key Organizations

### Northeast ALS Consortium (NEALS)

Founded in 1995, **NEALS** is the premier clinical research network for ALS in the United States and beyond.

- Includes **156 trial-ready sites** globally
- Has conducted nearly **80 high-quality ALS studies over 25 years**
- Maintains a **biorepository** of serum, plasma, CSF, whole blood, DNA, and urine samples
- Provides comprehensive training in outcome measures and site management, enabling rapid trial initiation

**Patient engagement:** The **NEALS Patient Education and Advocacy Committee (PEACe)** is dedicated to empowering individuals impacted by ALS through education and collaboration. As stated by PEACe: *"Nothing about us should be done without us."*

### I AM ALS

**I AM ALS** is a patient-led community and advocacy organization that has become a force in federal policy:

- Led by people living with ALS
- Played a central role in advocating for the **Accelerating Access to Critical Therapies (ACT) for ALS Act** (2021), which directed $100 million to NIH for ALS research
- Demonstrated that grassroots patient advocacy, amplified by social media and informatics tools, can influence federal research budgets

### ALS Therapy Development Institute (ALS TDI)

**ALS TDI** (based in Cambridge, MA) is a nonprofit drug development organization founded by patients and families:

- Operates as a full research institute, from preclinical studies to clinical trials
- Developed the **ALS Trial Navigator** — an informatics tool helping patients and families find clinical trials that match their profile
- Collaborates with the NEALS network and international partners

## Landmark Data Infrastructure: AnswerALS

**AnswerALS** is one of the most ambitious biomedical data projects in rare disease history:

- Enrolled **1,000 participants** from 8 sites across the U.S.
- Participants provided biospecimens used to generate **induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs)** and brain organoids
- Generated a **multiomic, longitudinal dataset** linking genetics, transcriptomics, proteomics, clinical data, and patient-reported outcomes
- Dataset is publicly available for qualified researchers worldwide
- Directly enabled computational biology approaches to ALS that would be impossible without this scale of data

**Stack mapping:**
- Technology: Cloud infrastructure for petabyte-scale omics data
- Data: Multi-omic integration (genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics)
- Information System: Data portal with researcher access controls
- Goals: Understand heterogeneity of ALS to guide precision medicine trials
- World: NIH and FDA collaboration on data sharing policy

## PRO-ACT: Democratizing Clinical Trial Data

The **Pooled Resource Open-Access ALS Clinical Trials (PRO-ACT)** database represents a historic act of data democratization by the ALS patient community:

- Created through a collaboration between **Prize4Life** (a patient-founded organization) and **NEALS**
- Contains placebo arm data from **11,675 people with ALS** across industry, foundation, and academic trials
- Freely available under open-access terms
- Has enabled **machine learning research** on ALS progression, biomarker discovery, and trial endpoint optimization
- Demonstrated that patient-centered data sharing can be more scientifically productive than proprietary data silos

## The HEALEY ALS Platform Trial

The **HEALEY ALS Platform Trial**, led by the Healey & AMG Center for ALS at Massachusetts General Hospital, represents the state of the art in patient-centered trial design:

- A **multi-arm platform trial** testing multiple therapeutic regimens simultaneously under one protocol
- Involves 78 participating U.S. sites overseen by a single SMART-IRB
- **ALS patient members serve on steering committees** — above-the-line governance participation
- Uses Bayesian adaptive design to accelerate evaluation of multiple agents simultaneously
- Designed with significant patient and advocate input on endpoints and trial burden

<div class="patient-voice">
"The platform trial model exists partly because patients and advocates demanded it. Traditional sequential trials were too slow when people were dying. We asked for a faster, smarter design — and researchers listened."

## Digital Health and Wearables in ALS

ALS creates unique challenges and opportunities for wearable and digital health:

| Application | Technology | What it Measures |
|---|---|---|
| Disease progression tracking | Wearable accelerometers | Functional motor decline |
| Speech monitoring | Mobile speech analysis apps | Bulbar onset detection, speech intelligibility |
| AAC (augmentative and alternative communication) | Eye-tracking, brain-computer interfaces | Communication after verbal speech is lost |
| Respiratory monitoring | Pulse oximetry, respiratory rate wearables | Home ventilation management |
| ALS Functional Rating Scale (ALSFRS-R) | Mobile apps / tablet-based PRO | Patient-reported functional status — primary trial endpoint |
